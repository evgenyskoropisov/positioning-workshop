# Mercuryo.io Source Notes

- Research date: 2026-03-27
- Source type: public official pages plus direct-competitor homepages

## Mercuryo official sources

### 1. Homepage

- URL: [https://mercuryo.io/](https://mercuryo.io/)
- Key signals:
  - Uses the headline "The payment engine behind top web3 business".
  - Says it is trusted by 500+ leading web3 companies.
  - Shows MetaMask, Trust Wallet, Ledger, 1inch, PancakeSwap, Bybit, Bitget Wallet, and Tonkeeper logos.
  - Presents product set across On-Ramp, Spend, Off-Ramp, Pro, DCA, NFT Checkout, and custom solutions.
  - States 12M+ KYCd users for On-Ramp.
  - Positions developer support around iFrame, API, and SDK.

### 2. On-Ramp product page

- URL: [https://mercuryo.io/on-ramp](https://mercuryo.io/on-ramp)
- Key signals:
  - Title says "keep users on your site while they buy and sell crypto".
  - Reinforces embedded usage rather than a redirect-first model.
  - Contact flow is enterprise and sales-led rather than self-serve SMB pricing.

### 3. Spend product page

- URL: [https://mercuryo.io/spend](https://mercuryo.io/spend)
- Key signals:
  - Says "Spend helps crypto wallets and Web3 companies issue branded payment cards to users".
  - Claims 150M+ merchants, 70+ cryptocurrencies, and instant card issuance.
  - Promises plug-and-play integration with wallets and fast go-to-market.
  - Explicitly says "No SDK. No setup. Just a link."

### 4. About page

- URL: [https://mercuryo.io/about](https://mercuryo.io/about)
- Key signals:
  - Mission: "We make crypto fit people's lives and businesses."
  - Says the company started with a wallet in 2018.
  - Says it became infrastructure-first for businesses in 2019.
  - Frames 2021 as the year compliance became a strategic advantage.
  - Frames 2024 as the launch of Spend with Mastercard.
  - Frames 2025 as the start of United States expansion.

### 5. Contact sales page

- URL: [https://mercuryo.io/get-in-touch](https://mercuryo.io/get-in-touch)
- Key signals:
  - Messaging is explicitly sales-led and business-oriented.
  - Product selector includes On-ramp, Off-ramp, Cards, Cryptoprocessing and OTC, Card acquiring, and custom use cases.
  - Industry selector includes Exchanges, Wallets, DeFi and dApps, PSPs and Aggregators, NFTs, E-commerce, Remittances and Payments, Gambling and Forex, Gaming and Community Platforms.

## Competitor sources

### 1. MoonPay

- URL: [https://www.moonpay.com/](https://www.moonpay.com/)
- Key signals:
  - Home positioning is consumer-forward: buy and sell crypto with many payment methods.
  - Also describes itself as the new standard for fiat to crypto.

### 2. Transak

- URL: [https://transak.com/](https://transak.com/)
- Key signals:
  - Home positioning is developer-forward: fiat on/off ramp integration for web3 and crypto apps.
  - Highlights broad asset and country coverage.

### 3. Ramp Network

- URL: [https://rampnetwork.com/](https://rampnetwork.com/)
- Key signals:
  - Home positioning is web3 financial infrastructure.
  - Promises users can buy and sell crypto without leaving an app or wallet.
  - Highlights a single verification and 8 million customers.

## Working inference

- Mercuryo is strongest when positioned as the embedded payment engine for web3 businesses, not as a generic crypto gateway and not as a consumer brand.
- The main positioning risk is product sprawl: On-Ramp, Off-Ramp, Spend, and other products can feel additive unless tied to one coherent business outcome.
