# Mercuryo.io: 03 Route Scorecard

## Route 1: Proof-led

- Best for: Enterprise and compliance-minded buyers who need a safe, credible partner.
- Promise: Work with a crypto payments provider already trusted at scale by major web3 companies.
- Proof: 500+ leading web3 companies, 12M+ KYCd users, since-2018 history, and known partner logos.
- What it excludes: A crisp reason why Mercuryo should win beyond being established.
- Main risk: Sounds credible but generic, and does not explain the product-system advantage clearly.
- Confidence: High.

## Route 2: Wedge-led

- Best for: Product, growth, and partnerships leaders at wallets and web3 apps.
- Promise: Keep users inside your product across buy, sell, and spend with one embedded web3 payment engine.
- Proof: On-Ramp explicitly promises on-site buy and sell, Spend adds branded cards, and Mercuryo offers iFrame, API, SDK, and enterprise onboarding support.
- What it excludes: A broad consumer mission statement and a vague "everything for everyone" platform story.
- Main risk: Multi-product scope can still feel blurry unless the wedge stays anchored in one partner outcome.
- Confidence: High.

## Route 3: Category-led

- Best for: Executive buyers and investors who want Mercuryo framed as long-term web3 payment infrastructure.
- Promise: Mercuryo is the payment engine behind the next generation of web3 businesses.
- Proof: Broad product set, enterprise sales motion, compliance narrative, and partner roster.
- What it excludes: Clear day-one buyer pain and a concrete reason to switch now.
- Main risk: Feels abstract fast and leans on language the category already overuses.
- Confidence: Medium.

## Comparison

| Route | Believability | Differentiation | Ease to repeat | Proof burden |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Proof-led | High | Medium | High | Medium |
| Wedge-led | High | High | Medium | Medium |
| Category-led | Medium | Medium | Low | High |

## Recommendation

- Best default route: Wedge-led.
- Why: It turns Mercuryo's breadth into one partner outcome: keep users in-product across core payment moments instead of stitching together separate vendors or redirect flows.
- What must be true for it to work: Mercuryo must keep the story focused on embedded buy, sell, and spend rather than listing products without a unifying problem.
- What to watch out for: Avoid vague terms like "gateway" and "cryptopowered" unless they are immediately tied to a concrete buyer benefit.
