# Mercuryo.io: Decision Log

## Current state

- Selected route: Wedge-led
- Workshop status: Ready for external review and variant adaptation
- Decision owner: Codex real-company validation pass
- Last updated: 2026-03-27

## Strategic decisions

| Axis | Decision | Why this won | Alternatives rejected | Confidence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Buyer | Product, partnerships, or growth leader at a wallet, exchange, or web3 app | Mercuryo's site, sales flow, and product examples all lean partner-first | Generic fintech and direct retail buyers were too broad | High |
| Trigger | The team wants embedded buy, sell, or spend flows without sending users off-platform or stitching vendors | This is the most urgent product and retention problem visible in the source material | Pure brand storytelling and "future of payments" framing was too soft | High |
| Wedge | One embedded payment engine across buy, sell, and spend | Turns product breadth into a concrete partner outcome | Leading only with ramps or only with cards made the portfolio feel fragmented | High |
| Proof | 500+ web3 companies, 12M+ KYCd users, on-site buy/sell promise, branded-card capability | These are the clearest public proof points available today | "Most trusted compliance stack" was too weakly evidenced | High |
| Category | Web3 payment engine | Matches the homepage language while remaining more concrete than "enterprise gateway" | Web3 financial infrastructure felt too generic and on/off-ramp platform felt too narrow | Medium |
| Boundary | Not a consumer exchange and not just a single-widget vendor | Keeps the story partner-centric and avoids product trivialization | Consumer everyday-life story is useful support language, but not the lead | High |
| Route | Wedge-led | Best fit between breadth, urgency, and competitive separation | Proof-led was safe but generic; category-led was too abstract | High |

## Open questions

| Question | Why it matters | Owner | Next check |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Should Mercuryo lead with On-Ramp or with the broader payment-engine story on first touch? | Affects homepage clarity and campaign structure | PMM or GTM lead | After comparing conversion on top-of-funnel pages |
| Which segment has the strongest buying motion today: wallets, exchanges, or broader dApps? | Determines ICP priority and outbound focus | Revenue leadership | After reviewing recent pipeline and win data |
| Is Spend a cross-sell story or part of the primary category story? | Changes how broad the hero promise can be | Product marketing | After partner interviews and sales-call review |

## Risks to monitor

- The message can become too abstract if "payment engine" is not tied to a concrete product outcome.
- The product suite can feel like a menu rather than a system if buy, sell, and spend are listed without hierarchy.
- Consumer-life brand language can weaken the B2B buyer story if used too early.
